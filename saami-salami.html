Saami Salami – building sovereign, human digital health

Saami Salami is the founder of Elisence, a digital health and AI platform focused on prevention, real-world evidence and human dignity. From the UK, he is designing Elisence as a long-term, sovereign health infrastructure that ministries, clinicians and families can trust.

Who is Saami?

Saami Salami is a UK-based founder working at the intersection of digital health, data ethics and everyday human life. His background combines engineering, law and hands-on work with patients, carers and clinical teams. Elisence grew out of years of lived experience with long waiting lists, fragmented records and the lack of a calm, trusted companion for ordinary people and their doctors.

In day-to-day terms, his work is simple to describe and hard to execute: build a platform that never replaces doctors, never turns people into ad-targets, and never forgets that behind every metric there is a real human being with family, history and emotions.

Some people write his name as Sami Salami, especially in informal contexts. Wherever it appears, it refers to the same person: the founder of Elisence and the author of its long-term vision for scientific, compassionate health technology.
