How Elisence thinks about EU MDR safety & transparent digital health design

This page explains, in a simple and calm way, how Saami Salami Asl and the Elisence platform think about European MDR/MDCG expectations—focusing on clarity, transparency and safe boundaries for non-diagnostic digital health tools.

This is not a regulatory claim. It simply describes design philosophy.

What EU MDR focuses on
Overview

MDR emphasises patient safety, transparency, traceability and predictable behaviour. Even though Elisence is not a medical device, the logic is shaped to reflect the spirit of these principles:

Clarity: The system stays within clear boundaries.
Traceability: Decisions and behaviour are inspectable.
Safety thinking: Features are gated, not automatic.
MDCG guidance
EU

MDCG documents highlight good practice for digital health and AI:

Explainability: Systems must be understandable.
Human control: Humans remain decision makers.
Data quality: Inputs and outputs must be coherent.
Where Elisence fits
Position

Elisence supports lifestyle, structure and health signals—not medical diagnosis. It sits comfortably within non-diagnostic boundaries and focuses on:

Insight: Helping families understand patterns.
Structure: Helping clinicians discuss trends.
Respect: Staying inside clear, safe limits.
Next steps
Info

Any future interaction with EU regulators would follow the formal MDR pathway through proper channels. For now, Elisence remains an insight and structure platform—not a medical device.
