UK context

Perspective

Elisence is being shaped with UK and European expectations in mind: clinical safety, data protection, clear accountability and the ability for regulators and clinicians to understand how the system behaves in practice.

Clinical thinking: Inspired by NHS clinical safety approaches (e.g. DCB 0129/0160).
Legal awareness: Designed to sit inside UK and EU legal frameworks, not around them.
Public value: Built so that ministries, hospitals and families could all benefit together.

Role in Elisence

Platform

As founder, Saami leads the long-term architecture and ethics of Elisence: how data flows, how features are switched on or off by policy, and how families are protected from noisy, overwhelming digital experiences.

Architecture: Multiple phases – from metabolic and GLP-1 support to women’s health and nutrition.
Governance: Feature flags, evidence packs and transparent rules for every module.
Boundary: Clear non-diagnostic, non-prescriptive posture – to support clinicians, not replace them.

Principles & ethics

Values

The work around Elisence is intentionally patient and careful: centred on human dignity, data minimisation, explainability, and a strong respect for the responsibility that comes with any health-related technology.

Human-first: Technology should support relationships, not compete with them.
Transparency: Designs are written as if they will be read by regulators and clinicians.
Healthy dopamine: The platform is built to avoid addictive loops and focus on real-world progress.
